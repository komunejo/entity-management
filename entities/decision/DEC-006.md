title: hard integrity is enforced by the engine; soft integrity is agent-assisted review

tags: [integrity, scope]

Hard integrity — types, required fields, enums, ID uniqueness, resolvable references — is *guaranteed* by the deterministic engine. Soft integrity — contradictions inside the prose ("died in winter" here, "died in summer" there) — cannot be caught by a schema; the skill provides an agent-assisted review workflow for it, honestly labeled as review, not guarantee. Keeping the distinction explicit avoids promising what cannot be enforced, while the prose itself stays the human-agent interface ([natural language stays the collaboration interface](../requirement/REQ-003.md)^[REQ-003](../requirement/REQ-003.md)).
